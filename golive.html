MADOO UX Case

These days, during my studies, I've been thinking about what project I could do to build my portfolio. What could a former healthcare professional offer in the corporate world of apps?

I thought about the challenges of the clinic and my years as an occupational therapist in mental health. One of the projects I'm proud of from that period was the reconstruction of the individualized therapeutic project (ITP), a document mandated by ordinance, created with the patient and the professional responsible for accompanying them in treatment, which guarantees the longitudinal follow-up of patients.

Six years ago I was thinking with the team about this document. We had complex patients, people experiencing homelessness, with complexities beyond health.

The problem: each professional put together the ITP differently! The social worker focused on social and financial aspects; the psychologists, on the therapeutic process; the occupational therapists, on routines, and so on…

So I created a document that invites both the patient and the professional to reflect on the various aspects of life. Inspired by the Canadian occupational therapy model (which suggests scoring systems for areas of life), I listed scoring areas and thought-provoking questions to reflect on treatment goals.

And then it clicked! What if everyone could have access to a tool to inspire them and think about their goals in the various areas of the complexity that we are? I'm not alone in this reflection. Several psychologists and people have already created something geared towards this, so I went further...

How to bring an engaging and attractive language so that people of any age can reflect on these points? You must have come across discussions about the excessive use of cell phones and gaming addiction. The feeling is that we do less and less and watch other people do things. A video of a recipe you're not going to make, or about an exercise routine you've been rehearsing for a long time, that trip that would be so much work…

Then the idea for GoLive emerged. An app that helps build goals for various areas of life. It tracks each goal and, in the end, produces a video of your progress. With rewards in stamps, shares, and a more active life offline.

Launching soon…
